Visualizing Information for Advocacy:

EXHIBITION | 09/05/2015 | Contributed by Reyna Clarissa


You may have detected your favorite typefaces on the iPad screens or admiring the nice papers at the array of printed matters in Brown 3 these past weeks. You’re talking about STET, an exhibition of publication design, curated by Janet Lu 16 and faculty member Isaac Gertman.

But what are you seeing really? These designers were up for the challenge to present lots of information within a set parameter that can engage well with readers while keeping in mind the hierarchy in their designs, carefully choosing elements that echo with the context. At the same time they are playing around with layouts, fighting against the traditional editorial composition. Their collective efforts result a diverse display of graphic styles, personal interests and design solutions.

From tasty Korean street food, the prettiest boy in the world to the evolution of debt, STET takes us on a visual vacation to a publication heaven — a typographic journey with good food for thought.

Reyna is a sophomore graphic design major from Indonesia.
